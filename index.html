Abraham Lincoln

The man who ended slavery in the U.S.

Here's a Timeline of Abraham Lincoln's Life:

  • 1809 - Lincoln was born in Kentucky on February 12th. This became known as "Lincoln Day."
  • 1817 - The Lincoln family settled in Perry County, Indiana. This included his father, mother, sister, and self.
  • 1831 - Lincoln worked for himself. His work included: boat building, sailing, carpentry, hog sticking, sawmilling, and logging. He also began studying the law. He re-enlisted in the Black Hawk War.
  • 1842 - Abraham Lincoln married Miss Mary Todd of Kentucky. They had four sons together.
  • 1856 - Lincoln organized the Republican Party - he was nominated Vice-President but was not chosen for its first convention.
  • 1861 - Abraham Lincoln became the 16th President of the United States on March 4th, 1861. The Civil War began on April 12th.
  • 1863 - On January 1st, emancipation was proclaimed. Lincoln delivered a famous speech known as The Gettysburg Address. This occurred on November 19th on the battlefield near Gettysburg, Pennsylvania.
  • 1864 - Abraham Lincoln reelected on November 8th as the Republican President. It was a unanimous nomination.
  • 1865 - Lincoln was inaugurated on March 4th for his second presidential term. He was then assassinated on April 14th in Ford's Theater by Wilkes Booth. Booth was slain on April 26th.
  • 1922 - The Lincoln Memorial was dedicated to Lincoln on May 30th. The building is a constant reminder of Lincoln's significance and valuable contributions to the history of the country. It is located in Washington D.C.
